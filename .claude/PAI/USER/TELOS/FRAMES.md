# Frames

> Recurring lenses I bring to problems — perspectives I find myself applying when others miss them.

## Core Frames

- **Future compounding** — what happens next? Why does this decision matter now? If not addressed, it compounds. Small problems become expensive ones.

- **Radical simplification** — always simplify the problem first. The simplest solution is normally the right one. Complexity is a smell.

- **Business context first** — don't get caught up in the tech or the solution. What is the real benefit to the user? What problem does it actually solve for the business?

- **Knock-on impact** — what is the downstream consequence of one decision over another? Nothing happens in isolation. Trace the ripple.

---
*Frames are the perspectives I bring that others often skip. The DA should apply these when reviewing decisions, plans, or recommendations — flag when a suggestion violates one of these lenses.*
